# Research Metrics And Channel Hypotheses To Explore Later

## Status

This topic is intentionally not formalized into an OpenSpec change yet.

Reason:

- we have identified it as important
- but we have not explored it deeply enough yet
- the goal for now is only to keep it visible so it is not forgotten

## Why It Matters

The current `ui_charter` and visual-identity work helps us explore:

- tone
- trust
- clarity
- distinctiveness

But it does not yet answer:

- how we will measure whether the landing page is working
- which channels should bring the first audience to it
- what kind of signal counts as meaningful traction

These questions matter before production landing-page implementation.

## Research Metrics To Explore Later

Future exploration should clarify:

- what counts as a strong signup signal
- what counts as a weak or vanity signal
- which qualification questions should sit after signup
- which responses indicate real pain versus polite curiosity
- how to compare results across audience segments or visual directions
- which metrics matter at this stage:
  raw signups, completion rate, qualified interest, interview acceptance, workshop interest, follow-up responsiveness

## Channel Hypotheses To Explore Later

Future exploration should clarify:

- which first channels are realistic in a French-first context
- whether early traction should come from local networks, content, communities, workshops, partnerships, or outbound conversations
- which channels best reach hesitant beginners rather than AI-native users
- which channels are likely to produce learning, not only traffic
- whether founder proximity in the Paris suburbs should matter for early trust-building

## Relationship To Current Work

Current visual exploration should continue without blocking on these questions.

But before a real landing page is launched as a market test, we should explicitly explore:

- research metrics
- qualification flow
- channel hypotheses
- experiment success criteria

## Reminder For Future Sequencing

A likely later sequence is:

1. finish the visual identity exploration lab
2. explore research metrics and channel hypotheses
3. define landing-page experiment criteria
4. implement the first real landing-page test
