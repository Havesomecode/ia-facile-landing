# Project Thesis, Market Discovery, And Success Criteria

## Current Project Framing

The project is not just "a landing page for an AI product."

At this stage, the landing page is a market-discovery instrument.

Its role is to:

- test whether a real need exists
- identify which audience segment responds most strongly
- validate which promise and wording resonate
- capture signups from interested people
- help discover product-market fit

## Core Goal

IA Facile aims to help non-technical people get at ease with AI.

The intended audience includes people who:

- hear about AI from a distance
- feel curious but do not know where to start
- are afraid of making mistakes
- experience AI as a black box

The broader transformation IA Facile seeks:

- move AI from something intimidating and abstract
- toward something understandable, calm, and part of everyday life

## Two Perspectives On Success

### From the visitor's point of view

The landing page should make them feel safe enough to sign up.

### From the founder's point of view

The landing page should help gauge whether the need is real and how to shape the right offer.

## Working Product Thesis

Current best articulation:

IA Facile helps non-technical people stop seeing AI as a black box and start using it calmly in everyday life.

This is not final copy.
It is a working expression of the current idea.

## Strategic Reframe

The strongest current framing is:

- not a pure software product
- not a generic AI startup
- not primarily a productivity promise

Instead:

- a reassurance-first offer
- a confidence-building entry point
- a practical bridge into real-life AI use

## Emotional Insight

The likely emotional core is not performance.
It is reassurance.

Possible emotional journey:

1. I do not want to feel stupid.
2. I do not want to make a mistake.
3. I want someone to make this understandable.
4. I want to see how this fits into my life.

This suggests a positioning that differs from many AI products.

Instead of:

- be faster
- be smarter
- automate everything

IA Facile may need to lead with:

- understand it
- try it safely
- use it in real life

## Landing Page As Learning System

The landing page should not stand alone.
It should be part of a loop:

Traffic source
-> landing page
-> signup / waitlist / qualification
-> follow-up or onboarding
-> insight

The project is stronger if the page collects not only emails, but also learning.

## What The Page Must Prove

The page should help establish:

- this is for me
- this is understandable
- this feels safe
- this seems useful in everyday life
- I can take a first step without being overwhelmed

## Current Hypotheses

These are working hypotheses, not facts.

### H1

Non-technical people experience real anxiety or hesitation about starting with AI.

### H2

A calm, reassuring, everyday-life framing will resonate more strongly than a technical or productivity-first framing.

### H3

People may respond better to guided help than to a vague promise of an AI product.

### H4

One subgroup inside "non-technical people" will likely show much stronger demand than the broad category as a whole.

## Possible Product Shapes Hidden Inside The Idea

There may be several different products inside the current vision:

### A. Education product

"Help me understand AI."

### B. Confidence product

"Help me stop being afraid to try."

### C. Practical utility product

"Help me use AI in my daily life."

Current intuition:

- the strongest wedge may be B leading into C
- confidence first, practical use second
