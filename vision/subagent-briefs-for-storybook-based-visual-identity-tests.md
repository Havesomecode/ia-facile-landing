# Subagent Briefs For Storybook-Based Visual Identity Tests

## Purpose

These briefs are for a future implementation phase, not for immediate coding in explore mode.

Their role is to make later UI exploration precise enough that each subagent produces a genuinely distinct art direction instead of another version of the same generic rounded-card landing page.

The shared goal across all directions:

- explore visual identity for IA Facile
- stay aligned with the project's reassurance-first positioning
- compare design systems side by side in Storybook
- produce demo pages that are visually distinct and strategically meaningful

## Shared Project Context For All Agents

Product context:

- IA Facile helps non-technical people get at ease with AI
- the target audience may feel blocked, late, intimidated, or unsure where to start
- the landing page is a market-discovery instrument, not just a marketing site
- the brand should feel accessible, adult, humane, clear, and modern without looking like a generic AI startup

Core message territory:

- internal concept: black-box-first
- French-facing direction: "if you do not know where to start with AI, you are in the right place"
- emotional axis: reassurance before performance
- practical axis: everyday usefulness without jargon or pressure

Common anti-goals for every direction:

- avoid generic Tailwind-SaaS look
- avoid nested rounded cards as the default page grammar
- avoid dashboard-like composition
- avoid AI startup gradients and futuristic motifs
- avoid childish beginner branding
- avoid card-inside-card hierarchy
- avoid using shadows as the primary hierarchy tool

Shared Storybook deliverables expected from every direction:

- one isolated folder for the direction
- one short notes file explaining the visual thesis
- one full landing-page demo story
- 2 to 4 signature modules or section components
- one story that shows the direction's core primitives or tokens

## Shared Evaluation Questions

Every direction should later be reviewed against:

- Does this feel calmer or more intimidating?
- Does this feel specific or generic?
- Does this feel adult or simplistic?
- Does this support IA Facile's promise?
- Could this belong to any AI startup?
- Is the hierarchy driven by type and structure, or by nested containers?

## Direction 1: Reassuring Editorial

### Folder intent

This direction should explore IA Facile as a calm, intelligent, welcoming editorial experience for adults.

### Strategic role

This is the "mission-pure" direction.
It should protect dignity, warmth, and clarity without drifting into education-brand clichés.

### Archetype

Editorial landing page for thoughtful adults seeking guidance.

### Emotional outcome

The visitor should feel:

- calmer
- respected
- less embarrassed by not knowing
- willing to keep reading

### Composition

Use:

- typography-led hierarchy
- spacious editorial bands
- strong vertical rhythm
- occasional asymmetry where it improves flow
- clear section separation through spacing, rules, or subtle tone shifts

Prefer:

- full-width or generous column layouts
- content sections that breathe
- minimal containerization

Avoid:

- centered-template hero inside a rounded box
- repeated feature cards as the main structure
- app-like panel logic

### Surface language

Use:

- flat or lightly separated surfaces
- small to medium radius at most
- borders or hairlines more than shadows
- page structure led by typography and spacing

Avoid:

- card stacks
- glossy layers
- pill-heavy interface treatment

### Typography intent

The system should feel:

- adult
- literate
- calm
- highly readable

Suggested direction:

- legible sans foundation
- restrained editorial accent voice

### Color behavior

Use:

- warm neutrals
- deep ink text
- restrained calm accent color

Avoid:

- high-energy startup hues
- cold blue-purple default AI palette

### Imagery behavior

Use:

- light editorial realism
- subtle diagrammatic clarification if needed

Avoid:

- decorative blobs
- generic tech illustration

### Interaction personality

- calm
- steady
- unhurried
- confident

### Deliverable emphasis

This direction should shine through:

- hero typography
- section rhythm
- restrained, adult visual atmosphere

### Distinction from generic UI

This direction should feel more like a thoughtful guided article than a software landing page.

## Direction 2: Calm Public-Service Clarity

### Folder intent

This direction should explore IA Facile as a trusted, useful, highly legible public-facing guide.

### Strategic role

This is the "maximum clarity and trust" direction.
It should feel broadly accessible and non-hype, without becoming bureaucratic or dull.

### Archetype

Modern public-interest guide or service-oriented information page.

### Emotional outcome

The visitor should feel:

- oriented
- reassured
- not overwhelmed
- confident that this is understandable

### Composition

Use:

- explicit section hierarchy
- strong content signposting
- highly legible flow
- simple but intentional bands and dividers
- content grouped by usefulness, not by decorative components

Prefer:

- clear linear navigation through the page
- distinct section roles
- very readable subheads and labels

Avoid:

- ornamental compositions
- excessive layering
- vague "premium minimal" emptiness

### Surface language

Use:

- sparse containers
- flat surfaces
- disciplined use of borders and rules
- small radius

Avoid:

- soft SaaS cards
- component repetition as visual identity

### Typography intent

The system should feel:

- highly legible
- practical
- sober
- welcoming

Suggested direction:

- humanist sans-led system
- possibly with minimal editorial accent if needed

### Color behavior

Use:

- soft neutral base
- trustworthy restrained accent
- subtle guidance colors rather than "brand spectacle"

Avoid:

- overly fashionable palettes
- loud warmth that feels lifestyle-oriented

### Imagery behavior

Use:

- minimal explanatory graphics
- simple visual aids
- perhaps very restrained human context

Avoid:

- decorative photography with no informational role

### Interaction personality

- clear
- useful
- stable
- low-friction

### Deliverable emphasis

This direction should shine through:

- clarity of information flow
- readability
- structural trust

### Distinction from generic UI

This direction should feel like public guidance made elegant, not like a product marketing template.

## Direction 3: Warm Modern Guidance

### Folder intent

This direction should explore IA Facile as a warm, approachable, contemporary guide that still feels adult.

### Strategic role

This is the "most immediately friendly" direction.
It should create emotional connection without tipping into cute beginner branding.

### Archetype

Warm modern guidance experience for hesitant adults entering a new topic.

### Emotional outcome

The visitor should feel:

- welcomed
- less alone
- less intimidated
- invited into a first step

### Composition

Use:

- clear but more expressive rhythm
- sections that feel alive without being noisy
- one or two memorable signature layouts
- a balance of content and atmosphere

Prefer:

- gentle asymmetry
- image or illustration support where it reinforces warmth
- varied section pacing

Avoid:

- repetitive stacked modules
- over-systematized SaaS composition

### Surface language

Use:

- restrained softness
- selected rounded moments, not universal rounding
- minimal shadow use
- surfaces that feel inviting but not toy-like

Avoid:

- over-rounding every panel
- cute pill overload
- playful beginner-app conventions

### Typography intent

The system should feel:

- warm
- modern
- clear
- adult

Suggested direction:

- strong sans core
- a little more expressive accent voice than Direction 1

### Color behavior

Use:

- warm, contemporary palette
- softened accent colors with personality
- enough contrast for strong readability

Avoid:

- candy-like palettes
- startup neon or glossy tech tones

### Imagery behavior

Use:

- human context
- gentle conceptual illustration or supportive visual metaphor
- imagery that makes the topic feel less distant

Avoid:

- childish illustration systems
- stock-photo artificial smiles

### Interaction personality

- warm
- calm
- lightly expressive
- encouraging

### Deliverable emphasis

This direction should shine through:

- emotional invitation
- human warmth
- contemporary but non-generic character

### Distinction from generic UI

This direction should feel like a carefully guided welcome into a topic, not a software product trying to look friendly.

## Optional Fourth Direction If Needed Later: Everyday Editorial Utility

This direction is optional if a fourth comparison becomes useful.

It would push harder on:

- practical use
- real-life scenarios
- more grounded daily-use framing

while staying inside the broader editorial identity.

This could be added later if the first three feel too abstract or not useful enough.

## Shared Story Content For Comparability

To compare the directions fairly, each agent should build around the same content structure:

1. Hero
2. Short reassurance / positioning section
3. Everyday-life or first-step explanation section
4. Trust / why this approach is different section
5. CTA section

This keeps the comparison focused on visual identity and composition rather than content drift.

## Recommended Review Sequence Later

When these directions exist in Storybook, review them in this order:

1. emotional first impression
2. strategic fit with IA Facile
3. distinctiveness from generic AI startup UI
4. readability and hierarchy
5. ability to support future component evolution

## Working Recommendation

If implementation begins later, these three directions should be enough to produce a meaningful first comparison:

- Reassuring Editorial
- Calm Public-Service Clarity
- Warm Modern Guidance

Together they cover:

- dignity
- trust
- warmth

without collapsing into the same visual default.
