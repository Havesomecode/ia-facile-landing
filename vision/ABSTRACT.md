# IA Facile Vision Abstract

## What IA Facile Is Trying To Do

IA Facile is currently being shaped as a reassuring entry point into AI for non-technical people.
The project is not trying to win by sounding more advanced, faster, or more technical than other AI products.
Its core bet is that many people do not need another AI tool pitch first.
They need help feeling safe enough to begin.

The working thesis is:

> IA Facile helps non-technical people stop seeing AI as a black box and start using it calmly in everyday life.

At this stage, the landing page is not only a marketing page.
It is a discovery instrument meant to test whether this need is real, which audience feels it most strongly, and what kind of first offer creates genuine pull.

## Main Choices Made So Far

### 1. Positioning

The strongest current positioning is reassurance-first.
IA Facile should feel like a guide or companion, not a hype-driven AI startup and not a generic training product.

The promise should move from:

- intimidation
- distance from AI
- fear of doing it wrong

toward:

- calm
- clarity
- first practical use

### 2. Audience

The first audience probably should not be "everyone who is not technical."
That is too broad.

The current best wedge is people who feel excluded, late, cautious, or intimidated by AI.
This includes especially:

- office workers who feel left behind
- cautious independent professionals
- adults who feel AI is "not for people like me"

The notes currently lean away from early adopters and optimization-minded solopreneurs as the first target.

### 3. Messaging

The most coherent messaging direction right now is:

- black-box-first
- with everyday-life relevance

That means the page should first make people feel recognized and included, then show that AI can become understandable and useful in ordinary life.

The strongest current message territory is close to:

> If AI feels like a black box, this is for you.

That should remain a working direction, not final copy.

### 4. First Offer

The current leading offer is a guided first-steps email sequence or short beginner program.

Why this is favored:

- easy to understand
- low friction
- aligned with reassurance
- realistic to test
- useful for learning what people actually need

The signup should ideally not only collect an email.
It should also capture a small amount of learning about what blocks the person today.

### 5. Visual Direction

The brand should feel:

- calm
- adult
- warm
- legible
- humane
- modern without looking like a typical AI startup

The current visual recommendations are:

- warm neutrals plus deep ink text and one restrained accent
- a legible sans foundation with a restrained editorial accent
- light editorial realism plus simple diagrammatic clarity
- a page rhythm led by typography, spacing, and structure rather than nested rounded cards

The main design anti-goal is drifting into generic SaaS or futuristic AI aesthetics.

## What Still Needs Validation

The biggest unresolved questions are:

- which exact audience segment should be first
- what the signup concretely promises
- whether the first offer should feel more like guidance, education, or accompaniment
- which traffic channel should be tested first
- how much human presence the brand and offer should show

## Practical Reading Of The Plan

The current direction can be summarized simply:

IA Facile is not trying to impress people with AI.
It is trying to make hesitant people feel that AI can finally become understandable, useful, and safe to approach.

The near-term job of the landing page is therefore:

1. make the right people feel seen
2. make the first step feel safe
3. learn what they actually want help with
