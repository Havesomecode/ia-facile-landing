# Visual Identity, Storytelling, And Page Narrative Directions

## Visual Identity Direction

Current belief:
the visual identity should probably not look like a stereotypical AI startup.

Likely avoid:

- neon futurism
- dashboard-heavy visuals
- abstract "AI" mesh gradients
- cold technical aesthetics
- "future" theater

Likely explore:

- calm
- warm
- legible
- human
- reassuring
- practical
- everyday-life grounded
- optimistic without hype

The visual tone should reduce intimidation rather than amplify it.

## Storytelling Shape For The Landing Page

The likely narrative flow could be:

1. Is this for me?
2. Can I understand this quickly?
3. Why should I trust this?
4. How could this help me concretely?
5. What is the first safe step?

This is not a final structure yet, but it is a useful narrative lens.

## Why Storybook And Playwright Matter Later

These tools make sense, but not as the starting point.

Possible later use:

- Storybook for exploring tone blocks, hero variants, typography, sections, and CTA patterns
- Playwright for validating readability, flow, friction, mobile behavior, and comparative screenshots

The idea is to use them to test expression, not to replace product thinking.
