# ui_charter Steering Criteria

Use this document to review the UI one criterion at a time.

The goal is to avoid vague feedback like "make it nicer" and replace it with explicit decisions that can steer future iterations precisely.

## Review Order

Review criteria in this order:

1. Composition
2. Surface language
3. Corners and geometry
4. Typography
5. Color and contrast
6. Rhythm and spacing
7. Hierarchy and signposting
8. Imagery and illustration
9. CTA language and shape
10. Motion and interaction feel

This order matters because later criteria should support earlier ones, not fight them.

## 1. Composition

Question:

- Does the page feel structured in a specific way, or just stacked?

What to review:

- overall page architecture
- section transitions
- hero layout
- asymmetry vs symmetry
- how the eye moves down the page

Good signals for IA Facile:

- clear structure
- calm progression
- visible organization
- not a generic centered SaaS stack

Likely current preference:

- calm public-service clarity

## 2. Surface Language

Question:

- What creates separation: boxes, rules, backgrounds, spacing, or type?

What to review:

- use of containers
- border behavior
- shadow usage
- filled panels vs open layout

Good signals for IA Facile:

- sparse surfaces
- borders and rules over shadows
- sections that breathe
- low dependence on boxed cards

Avoid:

- card-inside-card hierarchy
- decorative panels with no structural reason

## 3. Corners And Geometry

Question:

- Should the UI feel squared, lightly softened, or rounded?

What to review:

- panel radius
- button radius
- input radius
- whether shapes feel strict or soft

Current preference:

- more squared
- less rounded softness

This is one of the clearest current decisions.

## 4. Typography

Question:

- Does the page feel adult, calm, and specific through type alone?

What to review:

- headline personality
- body readability
- serif vs sans balance
- uppercase label usage
- density and line length

Good signals for IA Facile:

- typographic dignity
- strong readability
- editorial seriousness without luxury theater

Useful tension to manage:

- public-service clarity
- editorial warmth

## 5. Color And Contrast

Question:

- Does color create trust and specificity, or just atmosphere?

What to review:

- background tone
- accent usage
- contrast level
- whether color helps structure the page

Current steering:

- less fade atmosphere
- more clear, grounded contrast

Good signals for IA Facile:

- warm neutrals
- restrained accents
- confidence without startup gloss

## 6. Rhythm And Spacing

Question:

- Does the page breathe in an intentional way, or just use default padding?

What to review:

- section spacing
- internal module spacing
- pacing from top to bottom
- compactness vs openness

Good signals for IA Facile:

- measured spacing
- clear beats
- no bloated template padding

## 7. Hierarchy And Signposting

Question:

- Can a visitor tell where they are and what each section does immediately?

What to review:

- labels
- headings
- content grouping
- progression of meaning

Good signals for IA Facile:

- strong orientation
- obvious section roles
- useful labels without clutter

This is one reason the calm direction currently feels strongest.

## 8. Imagery And Illustration

Question:

- Does imagery clarify the message, or just decorate it?

What to review:

- whether imagery is needed at all
- diagrammatic elements
- editorial realism
- abstract visual support

Good signals for IA Facile:

- useful visual explanation
- minimal decorative noise

Avoid:

- startup blobs
- soft conceptual fades that weaken clarity

## 9. CTA Language And Shape

Question:

- Do the actions feel clear and trustworthy, or too product-like?

What to review:

- CTA wording
- primary/secondary balance
- button styling
- whether CTAs fit the page tone

Good signals for IA Facile:

- simple invitation
- no aggressive conversion language
- action shapes aligned with the overall geometry

## 10. Motion And Interaction Feel

Question:

- If motion exists, does it increase clarity or just add polish?

What to review:

- transitions
- hover behavior
- reveal patterns
- movement intensity

Good signals for IA Facile:

- subtle
- stable
- confidence-building

Avoid:

- drift
- float
- soft atmospheric motion that reduces perceived structure

## Current Priority Decisions

What already seems clear:

- prefer `calm-public-service-clarity` as the baseline
- keep some typographic dignity from `reassuring-editorial`
- avoid fades as a primary atmosphere
- avoid rounded UI as a dominant language
- move toward squared, ruled, organized structure

## Suggested Working Method

For each iteration:

1. pick one criterion
2. decide what IA Facile wants on that criterion
3. update one or more directions accordingly
4. compare again before moving to the next criterion

This keeps the system steerable instead of chaotic.
